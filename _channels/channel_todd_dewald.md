## About the Channel

Todd DeWald is a wildlife and nature photographer based in Connecticut who combines his background in fisheries and wildlife science with a passion for conservation education. After a brief career in law enforcement, Todd returned to school at Oregon State University to study wildlife science, which ignited his photography journey in 2020. As a former Forest Ranger and Crew Leader at McLean Game Refuge, he brings ecological knowledge and field expertise to every video.

## Content Style

- **Conservation-focused** education through photography
- **Four content pillars** - vlogs, gear reviews, tips, habitat series
- **Science-based approach** incorporating wildlife ecology
- **Field documentation** of Connecticut's biodiversity
- **Ethical practices** always prioritizing wildlife welfare

## Why We Recommend This Channel

Todd bridges the gap between wildlife photography and conservation science. His background in fisheries and wildlife provides ecological context that goes beyond typical photography channels. He reveals the hidden wildlife in developed areas, helping viewers reconnect with nature in their own backyards while fostering genuine conservation awareness.

## Best For

- Wildlife photographers wanting ecological understanding
- Those interested in photography for conservation
- Beginners learning ethical wildlife practices

## Key Philosophy

"My photography aims to narrow the gap between human uncertainty and wild animals by showcasing their natural beauty. I hope to use photography and videography for conservation efforts and reconnect people with nature and cultivate a genuine desire for its protection."

## Conservation Work

Todd works independently and collaborates with local land trusts to document Connecticut's flora and fauna. He creates virtual tours of protected properties, making natural areas accessible to everyone and increasing public awareness of conservation efforts in the region.
